Executive Summary

The same problem exists in Classic Billing as PSA. Across 91 configuration calls and 65+ hours of SA time, the same data is being collected session after session — product catalog, QuickBooks version, payment processor, carrier rate decks, GL codes — all of which Sales either knew at close or could have captured with 5 targeted questions.
Three configuration areas dominate every engagement: Bill Center / billing profiles (75% of calls), payment processing setup (72%), and product catalog build (51%). These three alone account for the majority of early onboarding hours.
QuickBooks integration is a recurring friction point. Appearing in 38% of calls — and causing dedicated follow-up calls in nearly every engagement — the QBO/QBD question (which version? credentials? GL code mapping?) is consistently unresolved at kickoff and costs 1–3 additional calls per client to resolve.
Product catalog is the #1 blocker. Clients arrive to onboarding without a completed product catalog in 100% of cases. SAs spend 1–4 sessions just helping clients map their products into Rev.io format. This data exists at sales time — it's what was quoted.

The Classic Billing Core Questions — What SAs Ask in Every Engagement

These are the questions that appear consistently across all four SAs and all client types. The Classic Billing equivalent of PSA's "Core 8."

Core 10 — Asked in Virtually Every Engagement

  • 1. What products/services do you bill for? — Service types, product names, recurring vs one-time, providers. Clients always arrive without a completed product catalog.
  • 2. QuickBooks: Online or Desktop? Do you use it? — Version determines integration approach. QBD = no direct sync. Credentials and admin user required before kickoff.
  • 3. What payment processor are you using? — TRX, Stripe, other? Are you keeping the same merchant account? Need merchant credentials before payment config.
  • 4. What are your billing cycle and invoice dates? — Bill profile: cycle start, lead days, due days, invoice layout (1-col vs 2-col), proration rules.
  • 5. What is your collections policy? — Days until dunning, number of notices, suspension triggers, disconnect thresholds, attorney referral process.
  • 6. What carrier(s) are you using? — SkySwitch, Windstream, Verizon, NetSapiens, BroadWorks, etc. Determines usage plan setup and CDR import method.
  • 7. What tax engine are you using? — SureTax, CSI, Avalara, VoIP Innovations? Any tax-exempt customers? Safe harbor rate?
  • 8. Do you have agents / resellers? What's their commission structure? — Agent list, commission %, plan type (flat vs tiered), reseller vs agent model.
  • 9. What email notifications do you want to send customers? — Welcome email, invoice notification, payment receipt, payment bounce, disconnect warning.
  • 10. What logo, branding, and company info should appear on invoices/portal? — Logo (183x110 JPEG), favicon, marketing colors, legal company name, billing contact.

Additional Questions (Complex Clients)

  • What are your GL codes? — Required for QBO integration; clients often need to pull their CPA or accountant into a dedicated call.
  • Do you need DocuSign / e-signature on contracts? — Contract template, signing workflow, which fields to auto-populate.
  • What is your migration source system? — TRX token migration, legacy billing data, starting balances, invoice number continuity.
  • How do you handle customer credits and advance payments? — Credit application rules, prepaid customers, refund process.
  • Do you have resellers who white-label your service? — Sub-instance config, reseller billing profiles, commission pass-through.
  • What are your usage rate plans? — Per-minute rates by destination, included minutes, pooling rules, overage rates. Requires carrier rate decks.
  • Do you need SSO? — Integration cost quote required; not included by default.
  • What integrations does your PSA/CRM use? — ConnectWise, Autotask, Zendesk — determines ticket/order workflow config.

Classic Billing Pre-Config Checklist — What Sales Must Capture at Close

Modeled after the PSA "Core 8." These 10 data points, captured at deal close, enable ~60% of instance configuration before the kickoff call.

Must-Have at Close (Block Kickoff if Missing)

  • 1. QuickBooks version — Online (QBO) or Desktop (QBD)? Using it at all? If yes, designate integration admin user and email at close.
  • 2. Payment processor — TRX or other? Same merchant account or new? Will they accept ACH? Credit card? Both?
  • 3. Primary carrier / switch — SkySwitch? NetSapiens? BroadWorks? Custom? This determines usage plan complexity and integration approach.
  • 4. Products & services list — A basic list of what they bill customers for (Voice, Data, Hardware, SMS, etc.). This seeds the product catalog — the #1 blocker in every onboarding.
  • 5. Tax engine — SureTax, CSI, Avalara, VoIP Innovations, or other? Existing account or new? Any known tax-exempt customers?
  • 6. Migration source — What are they moving from? TRX token migration? Legacy billing system? Excel? Starting fresh? Determines import complexity.

Should-Have at Close (Capture in Sales Brief)

  • 7. Agent / reseller model — Do they pay agent commissions? Reseller sub-instances? This enables commission module pre-config and agent list prep.
  • 8. Billing cycle — Monthly? Quarterly? Annual? Invoice date preference? Due date (net-30?). Enables bill profile build before kickoff.
  • 9. Collections policy — How many days before dunning? Do they disconnect for non-payment? This is configured in session 2–3 and is always re-collected.
  • 10. Customer count + company size — How many billing accounts? This determines whether bulk import is needed and scopes the migration work.
