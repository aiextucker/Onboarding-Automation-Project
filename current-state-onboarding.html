⚡ The Core Problem

The client arrives at kickoff to a nearly blank Rev.io instance. The first 3 sessions are consumed rebuilding information that Sales already collected — prior system, integrations, user structure, dispatch model. Only after configuration is complete can the SA pivot to the actual value-add: teaching the client how to use the platform for their business. That pivot happens too late, too far into the engagement, when client patience is already strained.

Section 03
🔍 Step-by-Step — Current Process Detail
✅ Step 1 — Deal Closes (Salesforce Closed-Won)
  • Opportunity marked Closed-Won in Salesforce
  • Trigger for the onboarding process to begin — currently manual
  • No automated handoff today; Sales manually initiates next steps
⚠️ Step 2 — Sales Completes Manual Intake Form
  • Sales rep fills out a basic intake form — done manually after close
  • Captures: basic client data, number of seats, primary contact information
  • Does NOT capture: prior PSA, accounting software, RMM tool, dispatch model, invoice sequence, SureTax, GL codes, user roles — all re-discovered in onboarding
  • No structured handoff brief — SA enters the engagement with minimal context from the sales process
  • No Fireflies transcript review, no Salesforce signal extraction
⚠️ Step 3 — Manual SA Assignment
  • SA assigned manually based on capacity scoring and availability
  • Deliberation adds delay between close and first contact
  • No batter's box rotation — assignment is judgment-based, not systematic
  • SA receives the intake form — has no broader context about the client from sales calls
⚠️ Step 4 — Sales Schedules Kickoff Call
  • Sales rep schedules the initial 1-hour kickoff call
  • No fixed SLA on kickoff timing today — gap between close and kickoff is variable
  • Instance is requested at this stage — SLA: same day creation
  • Instance arrives with ~10% default configuration — generic, not client-specific
  • No pre-configuration done before the client shows up
⚠️ Step 5 — Kickoff Call (1 Hour)
  • SA meets client for the first time — with limited pre-call context
  • First significant time spent re-collecting information Sales already knew
  • SA sets up the ongoing call cadence: 2–3 weekly sessions
  • Scheduling is client-driven — client can push back cadence based on their availability
  • Client-controlled scheduling means no fixed go-live pressure early in the engagement
  • SA begins identifying configuration requirements that should have been captured at close
🔴 Step 6 — Configuration Phase (Calls 1–3: Weeks 1–3+)
  • First 3 calls consumed entirely by platform configuration — no usability training yet
  • SA is building client-specific settings from scratch: company info, users, dispatch boards, integrations, invoice settings, billing config, tax setup
  • Most of this data should have arrived pre-kickoff — it's being collected live on the call
  • Configuration must be complete before SA can pivot to the platform's value: teaching the client how to run their business in Rev.io
  • If 2–3 week call cadence, configuration phase alone spans weeks 1–3 minimum
  • Client frustration begins here: "I thought we covered this in sales…"
  • SA methodology scores during this phase: 14–23/100 (should be 60+) — all three SAs score red early-stage
⚡ Configuration Bottleneck — What SA Builds in Calls 1–3
  • Company profile, legal name, billing address
  • User accounts with correct role mapping (reconstructing org chart live)
  • Dispatch board structure and service boards
  • Integration connections — QB, RMM, payment processor
  • Invoice number sequence and format
  • SureTax setup and tax configuration
  • Ticket types, statuses, and workflow structure
  • Business hours and holiday calendar
  • GL code mapping (often done without accountant present)
🔴 Step 7 — Data Import (Major Duration Driver)
  • Client must provide data in Rev.io's specific CSV format — customers, contacts, products, ticket history
  • Format requirements are given to client during onboarding — not pre-kickoff
  • Client begins "cleaning up" their data — this process takes weeks and extends the engagement significantly
  • Data quality issues surface mid-engagement, not pre-kickoff — scope gets re-defined on the call
  • SA cannot complete certain milestones until import is done — creates a hard dependency and a wait state
  • During the wait, client engagement can go cold — contributing to On Hold and RTS risk
  • No current SLA or structured follow-up cadence for clients stalled at data import
📘 Step 8 — Usability Training (Starts Only After Config is Complete)
  • Once configuration is complete, SA pivots to platform training
  • This is the high-value work: teaching the client how Rev.io solves their specific business problems
  • SA methodology scores improve significantly mid and late-stage once configuration burden is lifted
  • By this point, client has been in onboarding for weeks — patience may be thin
  • Late pivot to value means shorter runway for training before go-live target
🚀 Step 9 — Go-Live (~Day 60)
  • Client graduates to live on Rev.io PSA
  • Average duration today: ~60 days
  • Simple clients (small, no migration) shouldn't take 60 days — the configuration front-load inflates every engagement
  • Target under automation: ≤1 week for simple clients, ≤3 weeks with payment migration
Section 04
📞 SA Call Cadence — Current Pattern

2–3 weekly calls per client. Client controls pace — SA meets their availability. No fixed go-live milestone pressure during the session cadence.

Kickoff — Hour 1
Introductions + Discovery Re-Run
SA meets client. Re-collects information Sales already knew. Sets call cadence. Client sees blank (10% default) instance for the first time. Configuration requirements identified from scratch.
Call 2 — Week 2
Core Configuration — Company, Users, Billing
Company profile, user accounts and roles, invoice settings, billing configuration. GL codes often require accountant follow-up — creates a hold cycle. Client starting to wonder when they'll actually use the platform.
Call 3 — Week 3
Integrations + Dispatch + Tax Config
Integration connections (QB, RMM, payment processor), dispatch board structure, service boards, ticket types, SureTax and tax setup. This is still configuration — no platform training yet. Data import CSV format provided to client. The data cleanup clock starts.
Calls 4–5 — Weeks 4–6 (or later)
Pivot to Usability — Ticketing, Workflows, Portal
Platform training begins. Ticket workflow, service board use, customer portal, reporting. Data import still pending — client is cleaning and formatting data. Engagement risk increases here if client momentum has slowed.
Calls 6–7 — Weeks 6–8 (variable)
Data Import + QA + Final Config
Import file received (if ready). Data engineering validates and runs import. Final configuration adjustments, QA testing, go-live prep. Duration at this stage is entirely dependent on when the client delivered clean data.
Final Call — ~Week 8–10
Go-Live Sign-Off
Client confirmed live on Rev.io PSA. Handoff to support. Duration ranges from 45 to 90+ days depending on data complexity and client pace.

📌 Key Finding — 9 of 13 Milestones Are Pure Configuration

Of the 13 milestones, 9 are primarily configuration work that begins only after the client shows up to kickoff. Only 2 milestones (Quote Templates, Processes) blend configuration and training. Only 1 milestone (Finalize/QA) is pure training. The system is front-loaded with setup work that should be completed before the first client session.

Section 07
🎯 From 60 Days to <1 Week — What Has to Change
Before Kickoff — The Automation Opportunity
  • Sales captures the Core 8 at close — prior PSA, accounting software, RMM, payment processor, users + roles, dispatch model, invoice sequence, SureTax
  • Fireflies transcripts pulled automatically on Closed-Won — signals extracted before kickoff
  • Instance pre-configured using Rev.io API + browser automation — customer record, contacts, users, invoice settings, integration flags, SA context note
  • CSV import templates sent to client at close — not at kickoff. Data cleanup starts immediately, runs in parallel
  • SA brief delivered 48 hours before kickoff — pre-configured items, open questions, client context from sales calls
  • Revii AI enabled before the client logs in — Platform First, every time
At Kickoff — What Changes
  • SA opens: "We set this up based on your sales conversations. Here's what's done. Here's what we still need from you."
  • Session 1 goes straight to training — not setup
  • Client feels known and cared for from the first minute
  • Simple clients (≤$700/mo, no migration) go live within 1 week
  • Kickoff target: same day or next day after close — batter's box SA rotation, no deliberation
🏁 The Target State

Instance arrives at 60–80% pre-configured based on sales call intelligence. SA uses session time to refine, train, and handle the 20–40% that genuinely requires client input. Data import clock starts at close, not at kickoff. Simple clients are live in a week. Complex clients are measured in weeks, not months.

Early-stage SA methodology scores rise from 14–23/100 → 60+. Not because SAs get better at early calls — but because pre-configuration means early calls are no longer setup sessions. They're training sessions. That's the entire difference.
