Analysis
🔍 Key Findings
📅
Nicole got the early accounts — graduation count follows assignment order
Of the 11 accounts sold before Oct 2025 (the program's first ~90 days), Nicole received 7 of them — including 5 of the first 6 sold. First-assigned accounts naturally graduate first. This isn't a performance advantage; it's a timing artifact. Any SA given 7 accounts in Jul–Sep 2025 would show more graduations today than one who started receiving accounts in Oct–Dec 2025.
⚡
Kegan's velocity is actually the fastest — 75 days avg vs. Nicole's 111
When you measure what the SA controls — days from kickoff to graduation — Kegan averages 75 days across 4 clients (range: 61–98). Nicole averages 111 days across 11. Kegan is moving clients through onboarding ~30% faster than the current average. This is the more meaningful performance metric, not raw graduation count.
🐢
Long-duration outliers (240+ days) are the real signal — not headcount
iNet Communications (Jeremy, 240 days) and AhelioTech (Fran, 252 days) are the two longest-running graduated accounts. Both are SA-led, both were early cohort (Jul 2025). AhelioTech is a strategic partner so duration may be justified — but iNet at $315 MRR taking 240 days warrants a retrospective. What caused the delay — client engagement, complexity, or something else? That answer is more useful than the graduation count debate.
🔄
Nicole's Self-Serve accounts average 112 days — same as her SA-led (111 days)
Nicole handles 5 Self-Serve accounts and 6 SA-led accounts. Their average durations are nearly identical (112 vs. 111 days). This suggests self-serve isn't actually moving faster in her portfolio — which is worth investigating. Self-serve should theoretically graduate faster. Is there a client engagement pattern or bottleneck in the self-serve flow?
📦
Ataraxis Solutions: 156-day KO→Grad reflects hold time, not SA pace
Ataraxis (Nicole) has a 76-day gap between close and kickoff and a 156-day KO→Grad duration — but the account was placed on hold during that period. The hold time inflates the duration metric. This is not a performance indicator for Nicole; it should be excluded or adjusted when benchmarking her average velocity.
👤
Bullfrog graduated in 16 days with no SA assigned — needs cleanup
Bullfrog has no Solutions Analyst assigned in the DB and graduated in 16 days (sold Mar 5, KO Mar 11, Grad Mar 27). That's the fastest graduation in the dataset. Was this self-serve? Was it missed in assignment? Either way, the missing SA ownership is a data quality issue that should be corrected in Notion.

Next Steps
🎯 Recommendations
1
Reframe the metric: use KO→Grad duration, not graduation count
Raw graduation count is heavily influenced by when accounts were assigned. Velocity (days from kickoff to graduation) is the controllable performance metric. By that measure, Kegan leads the team at 75 days avg. Socialize this framing before anyone takes the graduation count at face value.
2
Do a retrospective on iNet Communications (240 days, $315 MRR)
This is the clearest outlier that doesn't have a strategic explanation. Was the client unresponsive? Was there a complexity issue that wasn't flagged early? Understanding what happened here informs process, not performance reviews.
3
Assign SA ownership to Bullfrog in Notion
Bullfrog graduated in 16 days with no SA assigned — fastest in the dataset. Fix the data record so it doesn't create noise in reporting. If it was truly unassisted, flag it as a self-serve success story.
4
Investigate why Nicole's Self-Serve accounts aren't graduating faster than SA-led
Self-serve should reduce SA time and accelerate graduation. Nicole's self-serve average (112 days) is essentially the same as her SA-led average (111 days). Is the self-serve flow requiring as much SA involvement as SA-led? Is there a bottleneck in the client portal experience or handoff?
5
Track assigned account dates alongside graduation dates going forward
To make SA comparisons fair as the team grows, the DB should capture when an account was assigned to an SA (not just when it was sold or kicked off). This removes the timing artifact and makes velocity comparisons apples-to-apples across the team.
