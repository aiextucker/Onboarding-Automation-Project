3. Confidence Language

High

“High confidence. The date is good. Remaining work is [work], scheduled for [date].”

Medium

“Medium confidence. Holding the forecast for now, dependent on [dependency] by [date]. If that misses, move to [new date].”

Low

“Low confidence. [date] is not credible unless [specific thing] happens by [date]. Recommendation: [move date / RTS / escalate].”

4. Blocker Language

Product / Engineering

“Blocked by [issue]. Owner: [person/team]. Client impact: [impact]. Clear path: [ticket/escalation/workaround]. Needed by [date].”

Client Engagement

“Blocked by engagement. Last meaningful contact: [date]. Attempts: [attempts]. Sales rep: [rep]. Recommendation: [next meeting / RTS / pre-cancel].”

Sales / Contract / Expectations

“Sales/expectation issue. Client believes [client expectation]; actual state is [contract/payment/product reality]. Sales owner: [rep]. Recommendation: [re-engage / RTS / pre-cancel] by [date].”

Data / Import

“Blocked by data/import. Missing: [data/API key/template field]. Owner: [owner]. ETA: [date]. Forecast impact: [none / at risk / move date].”

Training / Validation

“Configuration is mostly complete. Remaining item: [walkthrough/testing/Q&A]. Client action needed: [action]. Confidence: [level].”

5. RTS / On-Hold

“[Client] is [On Hold / RTS] because [reason]. Sales rep: [rep]. Last activity: [date]. Re-engagement owner: [owner]. If not cleared by [date], recommendation: [pre-cancel / cancel / keep RTS with reason].”

Operating Rule

  • Sales has roughly 2–3 weeks to re-engage RTS/on-hold clients.
  • If not cleared, move to pre-cancel.
  • If still not cleared after pre-cancel, proceed to cancellation.
“This has exceeded the re-engagement window. Unless Sales confirms movement by [date], recommend pre-cancel.”

7. Facilitator Prompts

  • “Percent complete?”
  • “What phases are done?”
  • “What is next?”
  • “What is blocking graduation?”
  • “Who owns it?”
  • “By what date?”
  • “Is this forecast date real?”
  • “Does Sales need to re-engage?”
  • “Stay active, RTS, or pre-cancel?”
  • “Has the latest client interaction been logged?”

8. Example Updates

On Track

“Prestige is forecast for May 28. Confidence high. 90% complete: billing, products, and ticketing are done. Final walkthrough is May 22. No blockers. Keep the date. Halle owns the walkthrough.”

At Risk

“Quick Copper is forecast for May 31. Confidence medium-low. Blocked by 403 permission errors when techs create contacts. Mark owns client follow-up; Ray/Engineering owns root cause. If unresolved by May 20, move to June.”

RTS

“Atlantic is not forecastable. Client keeps pushing meetings because the acquisition dependency is not ready. Sales rep: [rep]. Recommendation: RTS. If no movement in two weeks, pre-cancel.”

Workaround

“VanRan is blocked by subcontractor access. Workaround is one portal user plus tagging, with no added billing while Product works the correct model. Halle owns client communication; Ray owns permission guidance. Confidence medium until accepted.”

9. Closeout

  • Forecast date changes captured.
  • RTS/on-hold/pre-cancel decisions captured.
  • Blockers have owner + due date.
  • Sales rep visible for RTS/on-hold/cancel-risk clients.
  • Client interactions updated for stale accounts.
  • Product/engineering escalations assigned.
  • Month-end placeholders converted to real dates or low confidence.
