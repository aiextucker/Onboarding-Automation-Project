Overview
What Onboarding Is Designed To Do

Welcome to Rev.io PSA. Our onboarding program is designed to get you live and generating value from the platform as quickly as possible. Your dedicated onboarding team will work alongside you through structured configuration and training sessions to ensure your system is set up to meet your business needs.

Our primary objective is to configure Rev.io PSA based on what the platform delivers today — not to build new features or make custom modifications. The faster you can provide the right materials and attend your scheduled sessions, the faster you'll be live.

Before Onboarding Begins
Required Materials

The following must be provided before configuration work can begin. Incomplete or inaccessible data is one of the most common causes of onboarding delays.

  • Data Access
    API credentials or API keys from your current system are strongly preferred. If API access is unavailable, database access is the next best option. If neither is possible, exports are required.
  • Required Exports (if API / DB unavailable)
    Customer records  ·  Products  ·  Recurring services  ·  Tax data  ·  Inventory
  • Payments Application
    A completed merchant payments application along with three months of merchant processing statements. Required for payments underwriting approval. We recommend submitting this early.
Important: Not all data from your current system will be directly importable into Rev.io PSA. Fields that don't have a matching location in Rev.io may not carry over. Your onboarding team will work with you to find the best path for your data — but accuracy and completeness of what you provide is essential.
Your Responsibilities
What You'll Need to Do

Onboarding is a partnership. Your active participation is required throughout. The checklist below outlines what's expected from your team.

  • Provide a clear path to your data — API credentials, database access, or system exports delivered before onboarding begins.
  • Attend all scheduled sessions — Multiple sessions per week are required. Missed sessions directly impact your go-live date and will not result in license fee credits.
  • Review and approve configurations — You'll be asked to validate setup decisions during working sessions. Timely approvals keep progress moving.
  • Respond promptly to requests — Your onboarding team will send follow-up items between sessions. A same-business-day response helps keep your timeline on track.
  • Complete training on all modules — Including the web UI, mobile app, and Rev.ii.
  • Participate in final validation — A full system QA review is completed with your team before graduation to ensure everything is configured correctly and working as expected.
