SA Onboarding Playbook

The rules, timelines, status definitions, cancellation criteria, and escalation cadences that govern every PSA onboarding engagement. This is how we operate — not a guideline.

✅ Effective immediately ❌ Capacity scoring retired ❌ "Other" / "Fail to Launch" eliminated 📋 Owned by Onboarding + Sales jointly
Rule 01
⚡ Kickoff Timeline
Kickoff must be scheduled same day or next day. Always. This is not a target — it is the rule. There is no version of this process where a deal closes and kickoff happens days later.
🌅 Deal closes before lunch → kickoff that afternoon

The SA rotation assigns the deal immediately. Kickoff is confirmed and on the calendar before end of business.

🌇 Deal closes after lunch → kickoff next morning

Same-day is preferred. If not operationally possible, next morning is the hard ceiling. Not next afternoon. Not later in the week.

📋 Exceptions — rare, documented, and shared

Exceptions must be rare. When one occurs, it requires a documented reason and shared accountability between Sales and Onboarding — not assigned to one team. No undocumented delays.

Rule 02
🚦 Status Definitions

Every status requires a timeline and an owner. A status without either is not a status — it's a drift risk.

✅ On Track
Owner: SA · Reviewed weekly
Client engaged, milestones on schedule. SA owns and reviews weekly. No escalation needed.
⚠️ Off Track
Owner: SA → escalate in 24hrs
Client engaged but behind on a defined milestone. SA escalates to manager within 24 hours of identifying the issue.
⏸ On Hold
Owner: SA + Sales jointly
Client requested pause. Hold date is mandatory at time of status set. No hold date = move to RTS immediately.
🔄 RTS
Owner: Sales · 24hr response SLA
Return to Sale. Sales must engage within 24 hours. Clear re-engagement timeline required within 5 business days or account is formally canceled.
🚫 Canceled
Requires documented subcategory
No cancellation without a specific documented reason. See Section 03 for valid categories.
⏸ On Hold — What SA Does While Waiting

Being on hold is not passive. The SA drives active outreach on a defined cadence until the client re-engages or the account moves to RTS.

Day 0 — Hold set
SA phone call. Hold date documented. Sales copied.
Days 2–3 — No response
SA follow-up — phone first, then email.
Repeat every 2–3 days.
3 no-responses — Escalate
SA escalates to manager. Manager sends direct outreach noting account is at risk.
Sales is copied throughout.
Still no response — RTS
Account moves to RTS. Sales engagement required within 24 hours.
🔄 RTS — What Happens Next

Sales must engage within 24 hours of RTS assignment. If a clear re-engagement timeline is not established within 5 business days, the account is formally canceled and logged with a documented reason.

This is a shared accountability model. RTS is not Onboarding handing off a problem to Sales. Both teams own the outcome.

Rule 03
🤖 Automated Hold & RTS Cadence — Coming Soon

This cadence is system-driven, not manual. The SA does not manage a to-do list to remember when to follow up. The system fires on schedule. Sales and Onboarding share accountability at every step.

Day 0
SA phone call to client.
Hold status set with mandatory hold date. Sales copied.
Day 2 — No response
System triggers: SA follow-up email sent automatically.
SA notified. Sales remains copied.
Day 4 — Still no response
System triggers: escalation email from manager noting cancellation risk.
SA and Sales both notified. Manager email fires automatically — no manual send required.
Day 6 — Still no response
Account moves to RTS. Sales formally notified and assigned.
Sales must engage within 24 hours of assignment.
Day 10 in RTS — No re-engagement
Account formally canceled. Cancellation reason logged (see Section 04).
No exceptions without documented escalation to manager before Day 10.
⚠ Shared Accountability — Not One Team's Problem
Sales and Onboarding jointly own every On Hold and RTS account. An account going quiet is not Onboarding's problem to solve alone or Sales' problem to solve alone. If either team drops the ball at their step, the other escalates immediately.

⚠ The Rule on Complex Timelines
"Defined and reasonable" is not flexible. A billing client without a documented go-live plan and milestone dates is Off Track by default. The SA owns setting this timeline at JS2 and tracking it in the Onboarding Tracker from that point forward.
Rule 05
🚫 Cancellation Reasons
⚠ Eliminated Immediately
"Other" and "Fail to Launch" are both eliminated as cancellation categories. If a client cancels, it by definition failed to launch — logging that tells us nothing. Every cancellation must have a specific, documented reason that helps us understand why.
⚙️ Functionality
Must specify the exact product or feature gap. "Functionality" alone is not sufficient. Name the specific feature that was missing or didn't work as expected.
🏢 Client Initiated / Business Change
Must document the specific reason — going out of business, acquisition, budget cut, internal restructure, key contact departure, etc.
❓ Doesn't Clearly Fit Either Category
Do not guess. Do not force it into a category. Escalate to manager before logging. Every ambiguous cancellation is a data point worth understanding, not a box to check.

📊 On Hold & RTS Pipeline Standards

Total On Hold and total RTS counts must be actively managed to a high bar. The overwhelming majority of clients at any given time should be On Track. On Hold and RTS are not a normal operating state — they are exceptions that require active work to resolve.

If On Hold or RTS counts are growing, that is a process failure — not a client failure. The cadence in Rule 03 exists precisely to prevent quiet compounding. Any account that sits in On Hold or RTS beyond the defined timeline without documented escalation is a violation of this playbook.

💡 The Underlying Logic
The cancellation rate is a lagging indicator. The leading indicators are: kickoff timing, brief completeness at close, pre-config rate at JS1, data import delivery before JS2, and Onboarding Tracker status at end of JS2. Get those right, and the cancel rate follows. Every rule in this playbook is designed to move at least one of those leading indicators.
