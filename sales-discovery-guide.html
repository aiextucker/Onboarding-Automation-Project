These are the 8 questions that unlock the most pre-configuration. Ask these during the sales process — and as a structured 5-minute segment at the end of the closing call.

Section 03
📋 Secondary — Build the Instance Skeleton

These unlock the next layer of pre-configuration — get them in the sales process where possible.

💸 Billing & Invoicing 57% of calls
  • Do they have recurring billing / RMR? (yes/no)
  • Any tax-exempt clients? States with exemptions?
  • Are GL codes ready — or does accountant need to provide them?
  • Billing profile preferences: net terms, invoice format?
🎫 Ticketing & Service Boards 90% of calls
  • How do tickets come in? (email, phone, portal, all?)
  • Do they need a customer-facing portal?
  • Dispatch board needed? (field service vs. internal only)
  • Custom ticket statuses or fields required?
📦 Data Migration 84% of calls
  • Do they have a data export ready, or does it need to be pulled?
  • Rough customer count — how big is the import?
  • Parent-child account structure needed? (franchise, multi-location)
  • Data quality — is it clean or will it need cleanup?
🔌 Integrations 90% of calls
  • QuickBooks Online or Desktop? (Desktop = no direct integration today — flag early)
  • RMM tool: NinjaOne, Datto, ConnectWise Automate, or other? (captured in Core 7 — confirm here)
  • Outlook / Google Calendar sync needed?
  • Other integrations: PandaDoc, HubSpot, Zoho, PAX8, etc.?
  • Any custom API integrations required? (e.g. proprietary alarm monitoring systems)
🏢 Business Vertical & Service Model Gates config template
  • Primary vertical: MSP · Security Integrator · AV · Field Service · Multi-service?
  • Do they have field technicians? (dispatch board, mobile app, install projects)
  • Internal help desk, external client-facing service, or both?
  • Co-managed IT model? (affects user structure and client portal config)
📄 Quoting & Contracts 31% of calls
  • Do they use a quoting tool today? (PandaDoc, manual, etc.)
  • Contracts month-to-month or multi-year?
  • Recurring agreements / RMR driven contracts?
⏰ Business Hours & Holiday Hours Required for SLA config
  • What are their standard business hours? (e.g. Mon–Fri 8am–5pm)
  • Do they offer after-hours or weekend support? Different SLA tiers?
  • Which holidays do they observe? (sets holiday calendar in Rev.io)
  • Do holiday hours affect SLA response time calculations?
💬 SMS Capabilities Client communication config
  • Do they want to enable SMS notifications for their clients?
  • Use case: ticket updates, appointment reminders, billing alerts?
  • Do they have a business phone number to use for SMS — or need one provisioned?
📦 Inventory Management 55% of calls
  • Will they be using inventory management? (yes / no / future)
  • Do they track serialized assets? (equipment with serial numbers)
  • Do they use purchase orders today?
  • How big is the product catalog — rough SKU count?
  • Warehouse / parts van tracking needed? (mobile inventory)
📋 Project Management 56% of calls
  • Will they be using project management? (yes / no / future)
  • Do they manage multi-phase installs or long-running client projects?
  • Do they need milestone billing tied to project phases?
  • How many active projects at a time — small team or complex multi-project?
Section 04
⚡ If NOT an Existing Billing Client

Existing Rev.io Billing clients already have company info, billing profiles, and accounting setup in the system. For net-new clients, these additional items must be collected before the SA can configure billing.

📬 Send: Bill Profile Questionnaire Net-new only
  • Send the standard Bill Profile Questionnaire to the client before kickoff
  • Covers: invoice format preferences, payment terms, late fees, credit limits, billing contact
  • This must be returned and reviewed before the SA configures billing in the instance
📅 Accounting Periods Net-new only
  • What are their accounting periods? (monthly, quarterly, fiscal year dates)
  • When does their fiscal year start? (may differ from calendar year)
  • Do they close books on a specific schedule — affects billing cycle alignment
📎 Collect: Existing Quote Template Net-new only
  • Ask the client to send their current quote template (PDF, Word, or existing tool export)
  • SA uses this to recreate their format and branding in Rev.io before kickoff
  • Prevents session 1 being spent on "what do your quotes look like today?"
  • Note any required fields: line item structure, discount display, terms, signature block

Section 08
🎯 CEO Design Parameters — Non-Negotiable Constraints

From Brent Maropis to Fran Timothy, March 18 2026 — forwarded as design constraints for the onboarding automation pipeline. These are not aspirational. They are the operating parameters every workflow must be built to enforce.

⚡ Parameter 1 — Kickoff Timeline: Same Day or Next Day. Always.
  • Closes before lunch → kickoff scheduled that afternoon
  • Closes after lunch → kickoff scheduled next morning
  • Exceptions: rare, documented, shared accountability Sales + Onboarding
  • Capacity scoring eliminated — replaced with batter's box rotation (next SA in line takes the next deal, no deliberation)
  • Automation implication: Pre-config must complete before kickoff. Deal closes at 11am → environment partially built by 2pm. Minutes, not hours.
📊 Parameter 2 — Status Definitions: Every Status Needs a Timeline and an Owner
  • On Track — client engaged, milestones on schedule; SA owns, reviewed weekly
  • Off Track — behind on a milestone; SA escalates to Fran within 24 hours
  • On Hold — hold date mandatory at time of status set; no hold date = immediate RTS
  • RTS — Sales engages within 24hrs; no path back in 5 business days = formally canceled
  • Cancel — specific subcategory required; no undocumented cancellations
🚫 Parameter 3 — Cancellation Reasons: "Other" and "Fail to Launch" Eliminated
  • Functionality — must specify exact product or feature gap
  • Client Initiated / Business Change — specific reason required (OOB, acquisition, budget cuts)
  • Ambiguous cancellations escalate to Fran before logging
  • If a client cancels, it inherently failed to launch — that data feeds back into improving the process
⏱️ Parameter 4 — Go-Live Duration Targets by Complexity
  • ≤$700/mo, no data or payment migration → live within 1 week
  • With payment migration → live within 3 weeks
  • Billing clients → defined, documented, tracked timeline
  • Automation implication: The 1-week target is only achievable if pre-config eliminates Day 1 setup time entirely for simple clients
🤖 Parameter 5 — Automated Cadence for On Hold / RTS (System-Driven, Not Manual)
  • Day 0 — SA phone call
  • Day 2 — No response → SA follow-up email
  • Day 4 — No response → escalate to Fran + automated email from Brent flagging cancellation risk; Sales copied
  • Day 6 — No response → Sales notified, account moves to RTS if still on hold
  • Day 10 in RTS — No re-engagement → formally cancel + log reason
  • Sales and Onboarding share accountability — this is not owned by one team

Section 09
🧠 Knowledge Requirements for Pre-Config Automation

The API surface is accessible. The transcript intelligence is flowing. The missing link is institutional knowledge — the same tribal knowledge an experienced SA holds after 50+ onboardings. These are the 12 internal documents the automation needs to move from briefing the SA to doing the work before they ever open the account.

🔴 Tier 1 — Gates Automation (Can't reliably pre-configure without these)
  • PSA Configuration Schema Reference — ticket types and taxonomy, service board structure, user role definitions + permission matrix, SLA config options, order-of-operations for setup. Owner: Product / SA team
  • Integration Setup Guides — QB Online/Desktop, NinjaOne, PAX8, HubSpot. Required auth credentials, field mappings, GL account mapping logic, known gotchas, what can be pre-wired vs. requires client credentials live. Owner: SA team + product integration docs
  • Data Migration CSV Templates + Field Mapping Guide — exact CSV column structure for customers, contacts, products, assets, historical tickets. Field-level mapping from ConnectWise, Halo, HouseCall Pro, TigerPaw, Zoho → Rev.io. Max lookback window per data type. Owner: Data engineering team
  • Required API Credentials & Auth Flows Per Integration — exactly what the client needs to provide per integration (OAuth2? client ID/secret?), what can be pre-staged vs. requires live client presence, dev/sandbox mode availability. Owner: SA team
🟡 Tier 2 — Prevents Most Common Rework (Skipping these = second session wasted on corrections)
  • Client-Type Configuration Templates — standard starting configs by vertical: MSP, Security Integrator, AV, Multi-service. Default service boards, ticket type sets, user role matrix, billing cycle structure per vertical. Owner: SA team + Brooke Lee
  • Prior PSA Migration Runbooks — source-specific gotchas: ConnectWise, Halo, HouseCall Pro, Zoho, Autotask. What transfers cleanly, what needs transformation, what should be left behind, what the client must manually re-create. Owner: Data engineering team
  • Top 10 Rework Patterns from SA Team — what SAs fix in session 2 because it was set wrong in session 1 (e.g. GL codes mapped wrong, billing cycle set to wrong cadence, ticket statuses missing states). 30 minutes with a senior SA. Owner: Nicole Hills, Kegan Ehlers, Halle Taylor, Tony Mehner
  • Kickoff Session Agenda + What Gets Configured Live — which configurations are done with the client present vs. pre-built. Goal: SA shows up with 70% done, uses session time for the 30% that requires the client's judgment. Owner: SA manager / 13-milestone checklist
🟢 Tier 3 — Best-in-Class Outcomes (Client goes live faster, never needs to undo anything)
  • GL Code Standard Mappings by Accounting Software — QB Online chart-of-accounts → Rev.io GL code structure. Reference table: QB account type → Rev.io GL category → typical account numbers for MSPs. Would auto-populate 80% of GL codes before anyone touches the keyboard. Owner: Finance / accounting team
  • Product Catalog Naming Conventions + Category Structure — standard taxonomy so catalog doesn't become a mess 6 months in. Prevents organic growth with inconsistent names, wrong GL codes, and duplicate entries. Owner: SA team
  • Invoice Template Library + Billing Cycle Playbook — standard templates by client type (MSP monthly, project milestone, mixed recurring + T&M). Billing cycle guidance by revenue model. Owner: SA team + billing platform team
  • Feature Availability Matrix (Live vs. Roadmap) — what's GA, what's beta, what's roadmap-only. Prevents pre-configuring around features that don't exist yet (e.g. QB Desktop integration, sales pipeline). Owner: Product team (roadmap Notion page — read-only access available)
📋 The Ask — Four Conversations That Unlock This

1. Senior SA (30 min) — Walk through a typical onboarding: what you configure each session, the 10 things you always fix, what you do before kickoff vs. live with client. Unlocks Tiers 1+2.

2. Data Engineering (15 min) — Share CSV import templates and migration gotchas for ConnectWise, Halo, HouseCall Pro. Unlocks Tier 1 item 3.

3. Integration Owner (15 min) — QB Online field mapping doc + NinjaOne/PAX8 setup guides. What can be pre-wired vs. needs client credentials live. Unlocks Tier 1 item 2.

4. Product (async) — Share current feature availability matrix (GA vs. roadmap) so pre-config doesn't build around features that aren't live. Unlocks Tier 3 item 4.
