How we'll work together during implementation.

Phase I Deliverables

All Phase I items below are due within the first 30 days of the project. Timely delivery is essential — late or incomplete materials directly impact your go-live date.

Weekly Project Cadence

The project team will meet with your team for 1.5 hours each week, broken into one 30-minute check-in call and one 60-minute working session.

!

Attendance and engagement are required for a successful project. Decision makers should attend these calls so blockers can be resolved in real time.

Phase I — Items to Prepare

Due within the first 30 days.

Product Catalog

  • Product catalog in Rev.io format with all billable and non-billable products and features.
  • Catalog must include proper tax mapping based on your selected tax provider.
  • Rev.io does not consult on tax laws or perform filings — consult a third-party compliance expert.

CDR Formats / Rating

  • API credentials and documentation from each carrier — or a responsive point of contact at the carrier.
  • Live feed for each CDR/carrier and completed CDR workbook.
  • Rate decks, including domestic and international.

Merchant Statements

  • Three most recent merchant statements for all payment methods: AMEX, Visa, Discover, MC, and ACH.
  • API login ID and transaction key required to establish merchant profile.
  • Existing saved credit card / ACH payment account info and customer mappings for token migration.

General Info & Business Rules

  • Company logo in .JPG or .PNG format; 183 × 110 recommended.
  • Documentation of billing rules: cycle dates, late/termination fees, delivery methods, collections, etc.
  • End-customer support contact info — email and phone — for Rev.io to reference and route inquiries to.

Data Migration — Preferred Data Sources

Rev.io prefers the cleanest, most complete source available. Lower-priority options may require more effort and carry more risk of data gaps.

1

API Access

Cleanest, most complete, and most current data. Lets our team pull exactly what's needed on demand and validate against live records.

2

Database Backup

Full visibility into your data structure, relationships, and history. Best option when API access is unavailable.

3

Database Reports

Standard reports from your current system. Works well when API/backup access is restricted; may require additional reports.

4

DM Template

Final option — your team manually populates the Rev.io Data Migration Template. Highest effort and risk of data gaps.

Activation & Phase II

Going live and training.

The Activation date is when Rev.io notifies you that implementation has been completed per your signed Project Charter. Upon Activation, Rev.io Licensing Fees commence and you may begin sending emails and bills out of the system. Phase II trains your team and optimizes the system over the first two months.

Training & Modules

Included: Collections, Inventory, Workflow Processes, Request Templates. Optional: SYNC, Integrations, Analytics, Provisioning (Odin). Agents/Commissions are covered in Phase I.

Rev.io PSA

Brings helpdesk, project management, billing, and reporting into one AI-native platform. Phase II introduces PSA capabilities: service desk, time tracking, scheduling & dispatch, and mobile app.

Support Resources

Review roles and responsibilities of the Rev.io team and how/when to contact Client Success. Product documentation and training materials are available.

Train the Trainer

You “drive” configuration of additional settings to build confidence. Rev.io University, product documentation, and training videos are available throughout.
