Onboarding Guide

Things You'll Need to Know & Do

Your roadmap for a smooth Rev.io onboarding

Thank you for your business. As we begin onboarding, please understand that Rev.io may not operate exactly like your previous systems — to maximize the platform's value, be open to learning new processes. Your first step is a detailed kick-off call where your project manager will provide an onboarding packet. The list below will help you prepare for a smooth transition.

Phase I

Items to Prepare

Due Date All items in this section are due within the first 30 days of the project.
Product Catalog
  • Product catalog (in Rev.io format) with all billable / non-billable products & features
  • Catalog must include proper tax mapping based on your selected tax provider
  • Rev.io does not consult on tax laws or perform filings — consult a third-party compliance expert
CDR Formats / Rating
  • API credentials and documentation from each of your carriers — or a responsive point of contact at the carrier we can work with directly
  • Live feed for each CDR/carrier and completed CDR workbook required
  • Rate decks (including international)
Merchant Statements
  • Three (3) most recent merchant statements for all payment methods (AMEX, Visa, Discover, MC, ACH) for Rev.io Payments Quote
  • API login ID and transaction key required to establish merchant profile
  • Existing saved credit card / ACH payment account info and customer mappings required for token migration
General Info & Business Rules
  • Company logo in .JPG or .PNG (183 × 110 recommended)
  • Documentation of billing rules: cycle dates, late/termination fees, delivery methods, collections process, etc.
  • End-customer support contact info (email & phone) for Rev.io to reference and route inquiries to
Data Migration

Preferred Data Sources

Rev.io has a strong preference for how we receive data from your current system. Listed from most to least preferred — please provide the highest-priority option available, as your choice directly affects migration timeline, accuracy, and effort.

1
API Access Most Preferred
Cleanest, most complete, and most current data. Lets our team pull exactly what's needed on demand and validate against live records — minimizes back-and-forth.
2
Database Backup
Gives our Data Engineers full visibility into your data structure, relationships, and history. Best option when API access is unavailable.
3
Database Reports
Standard reports exported from your current system. Works well when API/backup access is restricted; may require additional reports to capture all fields.
4
DM Template (Client Completed) Last Resort
Final option — your team manually populates the Rev.io Data Migration Template. Highest effort and risk of data gaps; used only when no system-level access is available.
Activation

Going Live

The Activation date is when Rev.io notifies you that implementation (setup, configuration, training, conversion) has been completed per your signed Project Charter and Licensed Products are available for use.

Upon Activation, Rev.io Licensing Fees commence and you may begin sending emails and bills out of the system.

Phase II

Training & Optimization

Following activation, Phase II trains your team and optimizes the system over the first two months of use.

Training & Modules

  • Included: Collections, Inventory, Workflow Processes, Request Templates
  • Optional (if applicable): SYNC, Integrations, Analytics, Provisioning (Odin)
  • Agents/Commissions covered in Phase I

Rev.io PSA

  • Rev.io PSA brings helpdesk, project management, billing, and reporting into one AI-native platform
  • Phase II introduces PSA capabilities relevant to your workflow: service desk, time tracking, scheduling & dispatch, and the mobile app

Support Resources

Train the Trainer
